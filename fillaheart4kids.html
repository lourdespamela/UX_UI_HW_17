Executive Summary

The FillAHeart4Kids website contains outdated information that is organized in a disorienting and inefficient matter. It is not directly apparent what the organization’s mission is or what their projects entail.

Through 6 user interviews, my group members and I learned that in order for users to feel motivated to donate to an organization they want to know how their money will be used and feel a connection to the organization’s mission. The problem with the current design of the website is that the information is scattered throughout the website without an easy way to navigate around it to motivate users to want to donate or feel directly impacted by the organization’s website. As a result, FillaHeart4Kids is not receiving as many donations through their website as they are on their Facebook page.

My group and I addressed these issues by redesigning the homepage and highlighting the organization's mission and various projects so that users have information they can connect with. We also revamped the navigation menu by renaming the menu's categories and rearranging the subpages. Lastly, we streamlined the donation process so that users have control over what they donate and know what their money will be used for.

User Interview Plan

Research Question

How might we improve the navigation and layout of the FillaHeart4Kids website in order to streamline the user’s process when donating to increase donation intakes for FillaHeart4Kids and motivate the users to want to donate?

My group and I identified three objectives to collect information about when conducting our user interviews. Our first objective was to understand the user’s emotions that lead them to want to donate to donate and volunter. Our second objective was to learn about our user’s navigation habits and preferences. Our third objective was to gain insight about the user’s initial impressions of the FillaHeart4Kids website.

User Insights

Desire to Donate

We learned that users are motivated to donate to an organization when the process is simple and if they feel connected to the mission. We also learned that the current website does not meet these expectations for the users. See below for direct quotations from our user interviewees:

  • “Donation feature is too hard to find”
  • “The site could be more specific about its purpose”
  • “Mission is not prominent on the page”
  • “I want to learn more about how the money is put to use”
  • “I like to feel connected to the cause before donating”
  • “The donation process should be simple”
  • “I want to learn more about how the money is put to use”
User Navigation Habits and Preferences

We also learned some of the navigation tools that people prefer to use or that they hope a website has and what some of our users think about FillaHeart4Kids’ navigation system:

  • “Expects to be able to find information without struggling”
  • “Prefers specific and apparent navigation tabs”
  • “Thinks top menu bar is too small”
  • “The purpose of some tabs/pages is unclear”
  • “Looks for speed and clarity in navigation”
  • “Drop-downs are helpful”
  • “Good categorization/organization makes browsing easier”
Usability Tests

During our user interviews we also conducted 5-second usability tests from our participants to learn what some of their thoughts about the website are. We observed for pain points with the website and what they like about the website:

  • “Feels that there are too many different fonts”
  • “Color scheme is fitting because hearts are usually pink and purple
  • “Seems like the page is mostly event focused at first glance”
  • “Too many fonts/does not feel cohesive”
  • “It is not immediately apparent what the site is for”
  • “Site is very bright and vibrant”

Problem Statement

FillAHeart4Kids is a nonprofit organization that provides resources such as clothing, school supplies, and meals for children who are homeless or in foster care. They also provide opportunities for individuals to donate and make a difference in the lives of children in the community. We have observed that FillAHeart4Kids has not been receiving many donations through their website. How might we improve the FillAHeart4kids website to help a teacher who has several students who are homeless or in foster care feel more confident about making a donation to their organization online?
